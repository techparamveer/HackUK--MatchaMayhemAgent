# Matcha Mayhem email templates

Adapt these templates rather than sending them verbatim — swap in the
recipient's name, school or society, and one genuinely personal line about
why you are writing to *them*. Always British English.

## First contact — teacher / head of computing

**Subject:** Free for your students: build a first website, earn free matcha 🍵

> Hello [Name],
>
> I'm writing from HackUK, the youth tech education organisation that runs
> free, non-competitive hackathons and workshops for 16–18 year olds across
> the UK.
>
> We've just opened **Matcha Mayhem**, a free online programme where students
> build their own personal website and learn Git and GitHub along the way —
> most of them for the very first time. Everyone who finishes their site earns
> free matcha, and there's an in-person mini hack for those who'd like to
> build together in the same room.
>
> [One personal line — e.g. why their school/CS class came to mind.]
>
> No experience is needed at all, and it's completely free. Would you be happy
> to share it with your students? I'd be glad to send over a short blurb for
> your newsletter or noticeboard.
>
> Best wishes,
> The HackUK Team
> info@hackuk.network · hackuk.network · @starthackuk

## Follow-up (one only, about a week later)

**Subject:** Re: [original subject]

> Hello [Name],
>
> Just a gentle nudge on the note below — Matcha Mayhem is filling up nicely
> and we'd love [school/society name] to be part of it. If it's not for you,
> no bother at all; a quick "no thanks" and we won't write again.
>
> Best wishes,
> The HackUK Team

## Rules

- One follow-up maximum; a non-reply after that is a "no".
- Never invent dates, links or capacity — if it isn't confirmed, say so.
- Show the organiser the full draft before sending, and log every send with
  `log_outreach`.
